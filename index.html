About

I am a mathematician applying my geometric intuition to problems in artificial intelligence. After graduating with a first class degree in mathematical sciences, I spent five years in the City of London engineering risk and treasury solutions at Morgan Stanley, OpenGamma, and Barclays.

Seeking to align my professional experience with the mathematical demands of advanced research, I returned to academia. My interests focus on the foundations of machine learning, with specific interests in geometric deep learning, differential privacy, model plasticity, and uncertainty quantification. I'm particularly keen on understanding how to apply these concepts to real-world environments.

Outside of work, I apply the same endurance to my personal life as a keen cyclist and triathlete, having recently completed an Ironman 70.3 in Germany.
